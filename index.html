✍️ VOICE ACTRESS SIGNED · PREMIUM

Signed Specials (SP Cards)

The crown jewels of Weiss Schwarz collecting. SP cards are signed directly by the character's voice actress — personal artifacts, not just cards.

✍️ What Makes SP Cards Special?

Weiss Schwarz SP cards are hand-signed by the voice actress who voices that character. Pulled from boosters at ultra-rare rates (1 in 12+ boxes), each one is a unique intersection of fan art and collectible investment.

✍️
Real Signatures
Hand-signed by the actual VA — not printed, not stamped.
💎
Ultra-Rare Pull Rates
1 per 12–20 boxes. Some SP are 1-of-a-kind in graded populations.
📈
Appreciates Like Fine Art
Signed sports cards outperform unsigned. Same trend in anime.
🎌
Cultural Artifacts
When a VA retires, their signed cards become irreplaceable.

🌸 Start Your Waifu Collection

The hobby looks intimidating. It's not. Here's how to get started, spend smart, and build something worth showing off.

1
Pick Your Game
Start with ONE TCG. Pokémon Full Arts are most accessible ($50–200). Yu-Gi-Oh has cheaper entry via Ghost Rares. Weiss Schwarz has highest ceiling. One Piece is most exciting new market.
2
Buy the Character First
Pick a character you genuinely love. Emotional attachment is a collector edge — you'll weather dips without panic-selling. Markets respect conviction.
3
Grade Your Best Pulls
Don't grade everything — grade your BEST. PSA costs ~$30/card (slow). Check Pop first: if 500+ PSA 10s exist, grade premium is minimal. Low-pop + PSA 10 = value multiplier.
4
Store Properly
Perfect Fit → top loaders → vertical, cool, dry. Avoid sunlight. For best cards: hard cases or slabs only. Condition is everything.
5
Learn the Market
Track sold prices on eBay ("Sold Listings"). TCGPlayer for Pokémon. Mercari Japan for WS. Set alerts. Never buy on hype — buy on fundamentals and love.
6
Think Long Term
Lillie Full Art from 2018 = 10x original. Signed SPs from 2015 = 50x. Time in market beats timing the market. Buy what you love, hold what's rare.
